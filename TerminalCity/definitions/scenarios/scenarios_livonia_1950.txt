[description]
Flat farmland west of Detroit, cut through by country roads. The war is over. Veterans are coming home to the farms their fathers worked, or leaving them for the new subdivisions that are just starting to appear at the edges of Dearborn and Garden City. For now, this is still farm country. You can see it ending.

[backstory]
Wayne County, 1950. The section roads run north-south and east-west in the old township grid, a quarter mile apart. Most of the farms have been worked for two or three generations. The houses face the road. There's a one-room school on the main intersection. The cemetery at the north end of town has been there since before any of the current farmers were born. Detroit is 15 miles east. The new subdivisions are 5 miles closer than that.
